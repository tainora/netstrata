#### Decoding the Strata Committee: What and Who?

Previously known as the ‘executive committee,’ a Strata Committee is essentially the administrative backbone of a strata community. Comprising elected members from the Owners Corporation, the committee serves to facilitate decision-making processes on various aspects that shape the community – from mundane yet crucial tasks like hiring repair services to more complex responsibilities like approving renovation applications under the community’s by-laws.

The size of a Strata Committee can range from a minimum of one to a maximum of nine members, depending on the specifics outlined by the Owners Corporation. Furthermore, eligibility for committee membership is open, albeit with certain restrictions like barring building managers and individuals who have financial liabilities within the strata scheme at the time of the Annual General Meeting (AGM).

#### Beyond Just Decision Making: The Office Bearers

In addition to general committee members, specific ‘Office Bearer’ roles such as Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer are pivotal in strata governance. These roles entail specific tasks like meeting administration, record-keeping, and financial management, thereby shaping the framework that enables efficient strata management.

#### The Crucial Role of a Strata Manager

Often, Strata Committees are supported by Strata Managers—professionals skilled in aiding the Owners Corporation and the committee itself in streamlining the strata scheme’s operations. These tasks can range from conducting meetings and financial oversight to advising on asset management.
